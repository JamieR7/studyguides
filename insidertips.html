What to Expect: Sample Questions

The new 2026 exams are split into three distinct styles. Click below to see examples derived from your textbook content.

Paper 1A | Knowledge MCQ
Topic A.1: Which component of the homeostatic control mechanism is responsible for detecting a deviation from the set point?
  • A. Effector
  • B. Control Centre
  • C. Sensor (Receptor)
  • D. Negative Feedback Loop
‚úì Correct Answer: C
Explanation: The Sensor (Receptor) detects changes in the internal environment. The Control Centre decides the response, and the Effector carries it out.
Paper 1B | Data Analysis 3 Marks
Topic A.2 Scenario: A study compared sleep habits. Elite athletes: 49.5% reported "No problems". Sub-Elite: 25.0% reported "No problems".

(a) Calculate the difference in percentage between the two groups. [1]
(b) Distinguish between the two groups based on the data. [2]
‚úì Model Answer
(a) 49.5% - 25.0% = 24.5%

(b) Elite athletes reported significantly fewer problems staying awake compared to sub-elite athletes. Almost twice as many elite athletes (49.5%) had "no problems" compared to the sub-elite group (25%).
Paper 2 | Extended Response 4 Marks
Topic A.1: Explain the nervous control of ventilation during exercise.
‚úì Model Answer
1. Detection: Low pH and high CO2 are detected by chemoreceptors.
2. Proprioception: Proprioceptors detect increased muscle movement.
3. Processing: Receptors signal the respiratory centre in the medulla oblongata.
4. Action: Impulses sent via the phrenic nerve stimulate the diaphragm/intercostals to increase breathing rate/depth.
