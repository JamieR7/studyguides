A.3 Response

~25 Hours ~12-18 Marks
üí° Focus: Training adaptations, fatigue, and recovery strategies (EPOC).
Paper 2 (Extended Response)
Explain the physiological causes of peripheral fatigue in high-intensity activities. [4 marks]
‚úì Model Answer
1. Depletion of PC (Phosphocreatine) stores.
2. Accumulation of Hydrogen ions (H+) leading to acidosis.
3. Decrease in muscle pH affecting enzyme activity (e.g., PFK).
4. Impaired calcium release from the sarcoplasmic reticulum.
ü¶æ Theme B: Biomechanics

B.1 Generating Movement

~15 Hours ~5-8 Marks
üí° Focus: Levers (1st, 2nd, 3rd Class) & Planes of Motion.
Paper 1A (MCQ)
In a bicep curl (upward phase), which class of lever is operating at the elbow?
‚úì Correct Answer: 3rd Class
Explanation: The Effort (bicep insertion) is between the Fulcrum (elbow) and the Load (dumbbell). Most human joints are 3rd class levers.

B.2 Forces & Motion

~20 Hours ~10-15 Marks
üí° Focus: Newton's Laws (F=ma) & Projectile Motion.
Paper 2 (Calculations)
Explain how Newton's 3rd Law applies to a sprinter leaving the blocks. [3 marks]
‚úì Model Answer
Newton's 3rd Law states "For every action there is an equal and opposite reaction."
1. The sprinter applies a force backwards against the blocks (Action).
2. The blocks apply an equal force forwards on the sprinter (Reaction).
3. This reaction force propels the sprinter forward.

‚úì Correct Answer: Fracture from a tackle
Explanation: Acute injuries result from a sudden trauma (e.g., collision). Stress fractures or tendonitis are chronic (overuse) injuries.
üß† Theme C: Psychology

C.1 Individual Differences

~10 Hours ~5-8 Marks
üí° Focus: Personality Theory & Mental Toughness.
Paper 2 (Short Answer)
Define the term "Self-Fulfilling Prophecy" in the context of sport. [2 marks]
‚úì Model Answer
It is when an expectation (from a coach or self) influences behaviour in such a way that the expectation becomes true. E.g., a coach expects a player to fail, gives them less attention, and they subsequently perform poorly.
