Topic: A.3 (Fatigue & Recovery)

"Explain the physiological causes of fatigue during a high-intensity interval training (HIIT) session." [4 marks]
Key Points (1 mark each):
  • Depletion of Phosphocreatine (PCr) stores.
  • Accumulation of Hydrogen ions (H+) / Acidosis.
  • Reduction in enzyme activity (e.g., PFK) due to pH drop.
  • Disturbance of Calcium (Ca2+) handling in the muscle.
Tip: Don't just say "Lactic Acid". The IB prefers "Accumulation of H+ ions" or "Lactate accumulation".

Topic: B.1 (Levers)

"Which class of lever is the most common in the human body, and what is its mechanical advantage?"

A. 1st Class / Force Advantage
B. 2nd Class / Force Advantage
C. 3rd Class / Range of Motion Advantage
D. 3rd Class / Force Advantage
Correct Answer: C

Explanation: 3rd Class levers (like the bicep curl) are the most common. The effort is between the fulcrum and load. They have a Mechanical Disadvantage (force < load) but provide a Speed and Range of Motion advantage.

Topic: C.1 (Personality & Data)

Data Source: Table showing mean "Anxiety Scores" for Novice vs. Elite athletes.
Novice: 24.5 (SD ¬±2.1)
Elite: 18.2 (SD ¬±5.4)

"Calculate the difference in mean anxiety scores and comment on the standard deviation."
Calculation: 24.5 - 18.2 = 6.3

Comment: The Elite group has a much higher Standard Deviation (¬±5.4). This means there is greater variability (inconsistency) in how elite athletes experience anxiety compared to novices.

*Draws from ALL themes. Focus is on evaluating the study design.
Scenario: A study claims "Shoe X" improves speed by 4%. The study had 1 participant (n=1) and was funded by the shoe company.

"Evaluate the validity of this claim." [3 marks]
Points to hit:
  1. Sample Size: n=1 is anecdotal and not statistically significant.
  2. Bias: Funded by the manufacturer (Conflict of Interest).
  3. Control: No mention of a control group or placebo.
